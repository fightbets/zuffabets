## Design Context

### Users
Combat sports fans (MMA, boxing) who want insider knowledge and betting-adjacent content. They visit before and after fight cards, browsing on mobile during downtime or desktop during work. The job to be done: **feel informed and ahead of casual fans** — know what's happening in the business of fighting before it becomes mainstream news.

### Brand Personality
**Bold, Gritty, Confident.** ZuffaBets speaks with the authority of someone who's been in the game. The voice is direct, never hedging. Content is presented with fight-night intensity — unapologetic, opinionated, and deeply knowledgeable. This isn't a neutral wire service; it's an insider's perspective.

### Emotional Goals
**Curiosity + FOMO.** Every visit should feel like you're getting access to something others don't know yet. Headlines should pull you in. The design should create urgency without feeling spammy — the difference between "breaking news" energy and clickbait. Users should think: *"I need to read this before everyone else does."*

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference**: ESPN / The Athletic — premium sports media with clean layouts and strong editorial voice. Professional presentation that earns trust.
- **Anti-reference**: Cluttered betting sites with flashing banners, popup-heavy affiliate pages, or generic WordPress sports blogs.
- **Theme**: Dark headers/footers (canvas #111111) framing light bone (#FAF7F2) content areas. The contrast creates a stage effect — dark curtains, spotlight on the content.
- **Mode**: Current light content with dark chrome. No dark mode toggle needed.

#### Typography
| Token | Value | Usage |
|-------|-------|-------|
| `--font-display` | 'Oswald', sans-serif | Headlines, labels, brand text (uppercase) |
| `--font-body` | 'Inter', system-ui, sans-serif | Body copy, paragraphs, UI text |

#### Animation Timing
- Micro-interactions: 0.25s ease (hovers, color shifts)
- Entrances: 0.5s ease-out (fade-in-up)
- Ambient: 2-4s infinite (glows, floats, pulses)
- Scroll/ticker: 30s linear infinite

### Design Principles

1. **Fight-Night Staging** — Dark frames, bright content. Every layout should feel like the spotlight is on what matters. Use the canvas/bone contrast to create visual drama without visual noise.

2. **Insider, Not Influencer** — Authority comes from substance, not flash. Typography hierarchy (Oswald headlines, Inter body) should feel editorial, not promotional. Bold red accents for action, not decoration.

3. **Urgency Without Spam** — Create FOMO through content presentation (featured badges, ticker tape, strong headlines) rather than aggressive animations or popups. Animations should be ambient texture, not attention-grabbing interruptions.

4. **Mobile-First Intensity** — Most users are on phones between fights. Every component must hit hard at 375px. Desktop is the expanded view, not the primary experience.

5. **Premium Sports Media Standard** — Match the editorial quality of The Athletic / ESPN in layout and spacing. Clean grids, generous whitespace, strong typographic hierarchy. The content earns the design's trust.
